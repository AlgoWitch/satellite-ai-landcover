What Does This Website Do?

Imagine taking two photos of the same place — one from a few years ago and one from today. Now imagine comparing them to see what has changed.

This system does exactly that, but using satellite images from space. It helps us understand how land, vegetation, and cities change over time.

Why Is This Important?

Changes on Earth happen every day — forests are cut, cities grow, crops change. But it is difficult to track all of this manually.

This tool helps automatically detect these changes so we can:

• Monitor deforestation
• Track urban growth
• Study environmental impact
• Improve farming decisions

What is NDVI? (Simple Explanation)

Plants reflect light differently than buildings or soil. Healthy plants reflect more infrared light and absorb red light.

NDVI is a formula that uses this difference to measure how healthy vegetation is.

In simple words:

🌱 High NDVI → Healthy green plants
🏙 Low NDVI → Buildings or dry land

How This System Works

The process is simple and happens in steps:

1. You upload two satellite images (before and after)
2. The system calculates NDVI for both images
3. It compares the two results
4. It highlights areas where changes occurred

What Results Will You Get?

After analysis, the system will show:

• Areas where vegetation increased or decreased
• Percentage of change
• Visual maps highlighting affected regions

This makes it easy to understand what is happening — even without technical knowledge.

Real-World Example

Suppose a forest area was converted into buildings over time.

The first image (before) will show high vegetation, while the second image (after) will show less greenery.

This system will detect that change automatically and show how much vegetation was lost.
